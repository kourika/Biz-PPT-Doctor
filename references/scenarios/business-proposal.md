# Scenario: 商业提案 (Business Proposals)

Covers: 产品方案 (product proposals), 合作提案 (partnership proposals), 预算申请 (budget requests). Unlike internal status reports, these are persuasive documents — the audience has to be moved to a decision (approve, fund, partner), not just informed.

## Structure

1. **开场用 SCQA**：先建立双方都同意的现状，再引出问题/机会，最后给出提案的核心主张 — 不要用"感谢大家参加今天的会议"这类开场消耗第一页
2. **提案本身：一句话说清楚**，作为一张独立的slide，越早出现越好（不要让决策者等到第10页才知道你到底在提议什么）
3. **为什么是现在**：时机论证——不做这件事的代价，或窗口期的证据
4. **怎么做**：方案细节，但只到决策者需要判断可行性的深度，不要把执行细节做成决策依据的替代品
5. **要多少资源/要什么决定**：明确写出你需要对方做什么——预算数字、批准事项、合作条款——不要含糊带过
6. **风险与应对**：主动列出决策者会想到的顾虑，好过让他们在Q&A环节才提出来

## Preempt the objection, don't wait for it

A proposal deck that only presents upside invites the audience to spend the whole meeting finding the catch themselves. Include a slide (or a clearly marked section) addressing the 2-3 most likely objections a reasonable skeptic would raise — cost, timeline risk, why not the alternative. This is different from a workplace-report deck, where minimizing weaknesses is more acceptable; here it actively hurts credibility.

## Data credibility matters more here than anywhere else

Proposal decks get scrutinized harder than status updates — an unsourced number or a suspiciously round projection undermines the entire pitch, not just that one slide. Every projection should distinguish clearly between "this already happened" (verified data) and "this is our estimate" (assumption, ideally with the assumption stated). See `chart-selection.md` for chart-level data integrity checks.

## Length and pacing

Match slide count to actual decision-making time available, not to how much material exists. A 20-minute proposal meeting rarely survives a 40-slide deck — cut supporting detail into an appendix ("backup slides") rather than the main flow, and only pull them up if asked.

## Design conventions

Unlike internal reports, visual polish here is doing real persuasive work — but polish should support the argument, not distract from it. Avoid generic stock imagery (see `chinese-typography.md`'s "AI味" section) in favor of real product screenshots, real data, or real photos from the actual business whenever available.

# Logic Frameworks

Two complementary tools for structuring content before it becomes slides. Use SCQA to open a deck or section; use the Pyramid Principle to organize everything underneath.

## SCQA (for openings and section intros)

- **S — Situation**: a fact both sides already agree on. Neutral, not yet the point.
- **C — Complication**: what changed, or what's at risk, that makes this worth 10 minutes of someone's time.
- **Q — Question**: the question the complication raises, stated or implied.
- **A — Answer**: the governing message of the whole deck — this is also roughly your slide 2 or 3 takeaway, and the thing the ghost-deck test checks for.

Bad openings restate the topic ("今天汇报Q3的运营数据"). SCQA openings state why the audience should care in the next ten minutes.

## Pyramid Principle (for the body)

Every claim in the deck should answer "why?" or "so what?" to the claim above it — never just "and also."
